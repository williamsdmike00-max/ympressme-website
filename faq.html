🔥 DTF Transfers

DTF (Direct-to-Film) is a printing process where your design is printed onto a special PET film using water-based inks, then coated with hot-melt adhesive powder. The finished transfer is applied to fabric using a heat press. It produces vibrant, full-color prints with a soft hand-feel that work on virtually any fabric type.
DTF transfers work on almost any fabric: 100% cotton, polyester, poly-cotton blends, nylon, denim, leather, canvas, and more. Unlike HTV or screen printing, DTF doesn't require a specific fabric composition — it bonds to the textile fibers directly. It works on both light and dark garments without any special pre-treatment.
When properly applied and cared for, DTF transfers are extremely durable — lasting 50+ wash cycles without significant fading or cracking. For best results, wash garments inside-out in cold water and avoid high-heat dryers. Do not iron directly over the print.

🗂️ Gang Sheets

A gang sheet is a large transfer sheet (e.g. 22"×36") where multiple designs are printed together on a single piece of film. Instead of paying per individual transfer, you pay per sheet and can fill it with as many designs as will fit. It's the most cost-effective way to order a variety of designs.
Our Gang Sheet Builder lets you visually arrange designs on a virtual sheet before submitting. Here's how:
  1. Select your sheet size (13×19, 22×24, or 22×36)
  2. Choose a transfer size from the panel
  3. Click the canvas to place a design, or drag to reposition
  4. Repeat for all your designs
  5. Click "Add to Quote" to populate the order form
The fill meter shows what percentage of the sheet is used — aim for 80%+ to maximize value.

🎨 Artwork & File Requirements

Preferred: PNG with transparent background
Also accepted: PDF (vector preferred), SVG
Avoid: JPEG — it doesn't support transparency and uses lossy compression that degrades print quality.

For gang sheets with multiple designs, you can bundle all files in a ZIP archive.
Minimum: 300 DPI at the final print size
Recommended: 300–600 DPI

Important: resolution is only meaningful at the print size. A 300 DPI image sized at 4"×4" is great for a pocket print but won't look sharp at 11"×15". Always set your canvas to the actual print dimensions when creating your art.
Yes — for DTF transfers, your design should have a transparent background (not white). Here's why: DTF transfers the exact pixels in your file. A white background will print as white on the garment, which is usually not what you want. Only include the parts of the design that you want printed. Use PNG format which supports transparency.

♨️ Pressing & Care Instructions

Temperature: 300–320°F (149–160°C)
Pressure: Medium-firm (40–60 PSI)
Time: 15–20 seconds
Peel: Warm or cold peel — gently peel the film at a 45° angle

Always pre-press your garment for 3–5 seconds first to remove moisture and wrinkles. A smooth, dry surface ensures the best adhesion.
A Cricut EasyPress 2 or 3 can work for smaller transfers (up to 8"×8") if set to the correct temperature with firm, even pressure. A household iron is not recommended — it doesn't maintain consistent temperature or pressure across the design, which often results in poor adhesion and peeling edges. For best results and durability, a commercial heat press is strongly recommended.
  • Wash inside-out in cold water (gentle cycle)
  • Avoid bleach and fabric softeners
  • Tumble dry on low or hang to dry
  • Do not iron directly over the printed area
  • Do not dry clean
Following these care instructions will keep your prints vibrant for 50+ washes.
